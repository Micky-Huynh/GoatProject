Career stat vectors

What it is and what it does

Every player is represented by an 11-number career vector in stat space. Each number is an era-adjusted z-score for one skill or impact stat (BPM, VORP, PER, win shares, shooting, playmaking, defense, etc.). This vector is the shared input for rankings, maps, heatmaps, and most scores on the site.

How we calculate career z-scores

For each season and position group, raw stats are oriented (higher-is-better or inverted for turnovers), then z-scored against the full league that year:

zf = (xf − μf,group) / σf,group

Career value for feature f is the unweighted mean of season z-scores (seasons with ≥ 200 minutes). The career vector is:

x = (zBPM, zVORP, zPER, zWS, … ) ∈ ℝ11

The allowlist of curated players is filtered after league-wide adjustment — z-scores are not recomputed only among legends.

GOAT ranking (bar chart)

What it is and what it does

The bar chart on the Overview page ranks allowlist players by a single composite GOAT score. #1 at the top is the highest rank on this model. It blends unusual stat profiles, championship credit, and a clutch/consensus adjustment — not any one box-score line.

How we calculate score_goat_index

score_goat_index = 1.0 × score_pca_whitened_l2
                    − 0.35 × championship_net
                    + 0.45 × clutch_penalty

  • score_pca_whitened_l2 — distance from the multivariate center in PCA-whitened space (how extreme the 11-stat profile is).
  • championship_net — ring credit minus finals-loss debit, built from team strength and playoff depth.
  • clutch_penalty — tax when stat dominance runs ahead of playoff success and peer awards.

Lower composite scores rank higher on the chart. Use rank order (#1, #2, …) to compare players — bar length is the composite number, not a simple quality meter.

PCA map & 3D explorer

What it is and what it does

PCA (principal component analysis) projects each player’s 11-stat vector onto 2D or 3D axes so you can see who plays a similar style or sits in a similar part of stat space. Nearby dots share profiles; distance on the chart is not “better vs worse.”

How we calculate PCA coordinates

PCA is fit on all full-league career vectors, then each player is projected onto the top components:

PCm = (x − μ)⊤ vm

where vm is the m-th principal axis. The 2D scatter uses (PC1, PC2); the 3D explorer uses (PC1, PC2, PC3) — together about 65.4% of variance.

PC1 tends to capture overall impact loading; PC2 and PC3 often reflect style (spacing, playmaking vs rim protection). Axes are not universal good/bad directions.

Impact crown (3D explorer)

What it is and what it does

In the interactive 3D view, the gold crown marks the player with the highest overall impact among the players you currently have visible. Orb size also scales with this impact score. Titles and the bar-chart index do not affect the crown.

How we calculate impact_z

impact_z = ¼ ( zBPM + zVORP + zPER + zWS )

Crown holder = argmax impact_z over the visible checkbox set.

Similarity heatmap

What it is and what it does

The heatmap shows play-style similarity between pairs of allowlist players. Bright cells mean profiles point in a similar direction in stat space; it does not rank greatness.

How we calculate cosine similarity

Each career vector is L2-normalized, then pairwise dot products give similarity in [−1, 1]:

similarity(i, j) = x̂i⊤ x̂j,   x̂ = x / ||x||₂

Alchemy Lab

What it is and what it does

Alchemy blends two players into a hypothetical stat profile in an extended ℝ18 space (11 core stats + showman excitement + six shot-zone z-scores), then finds the nearest real allowlist player. Exploratory only — it does not change GOAT rankings.

How we calculate the blend and discovery

Combine with blend weight α from the slider (default 0.5):

C(u, v) = α·u + (1−α)·v   in ℝ18

Discovery — nearest neighbor by Euclidean distance:

D(w) = argminp || w − zp ||₂

Orb positions use PCA of the 11 core stats; the reported L2 distance uses the full 18-dim alchemy vector. See the Alchemy Lab “Math explanation” button for a worked example.

Exploratory layout only. Gold crown = #1 overall impact among currently visible players (mean BPM/VORP/PER/WS z). Bar chart uses score_goat_index (PCA + team-weighted titles + clutch/consensus adjustment).
